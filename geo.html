What's a Webring?

A webring was a simple way for communities online to share each other's work. Before the age of shares and likes, people would link to eachothers websites — Not only would they link to each other, they would organise the linking in such a way that each person in the webring would link to a unique member. This way, nobody was left out!

The organiser of a webring was called a 'ringmaster', but webrings were often organised
using an external webring provider services, which would allow you to embed a widget into your page.

The Personal Fan-Page

    Many pages on the early internet were like this one, they were quite
static affairs. HTML and its standards at the time was different
and much simpler, but this simplicity was approachable for the millions of people coming
online through the late 90's and early 00's.

    What they lacked in interactivity and function, they more than made up for in
personality. These websites with garish colours and animated gifs were, for many,
what the internet was. GeoCities facilitated this earnestness by providing a
platform for people to do whatever they want with web-design and explore their
interests. This level of cusomisation feels much more personal than what online
social interaction has become today.

    Though I only caught the tail end of it personally, browsing through
long-abandoned GeoCities pages and personal websites gives me a much more
full understanding of what the creator was all about than any social-media
page ever has.

When GeoCities was discontinued in 2009, something special was certainly lost.
